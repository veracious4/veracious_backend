 This is known as a Sure Start Maternity Grant.
 \n\n \n If you live in Scotland you cannot get a Sure Start Maternity Grant.
 You can apply for a Pregnancy and Baby Payment instead.
 \n \n\n You usually qualify for the grant if both of the following apply: \n\n \n you’re expecting your first child, or you’re expecting a multiple birth (such as twins) and have children already \n you or your partner already get certain benefits \n \n \n\n You must claim the grant within 11 weeks of the baby’s due date or within 6 months after the baby’s birth.
 \n\n You do not have to pay the grant back and it will not affect your other benefits or tax credits.
 \n\n \n This guide is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg) .
 \n\n \n You may not get a grant if you already have children.
 \n \n\n If you already have children under 16 \n\n You can only get a grant if at least one of the following applies: \n\n \n you’re expecting a multiple birth (such as twins) \n the child you’re caring for is someone else’s \n \n you’ve been granted refugee status or humanitarian protection and you have a child or children from before you left your country \n \n\n \n \n  \n  Children under 16 \n  Grant if you have twins \n  Grant if you have triplets \n  \n \n \n  \n  You have 1 or more (and none of them are from multiple births) \n  £500 \n  £1,000 \n  \n  \n  You’ve already had twins \n  £0 \n  £500 \n  \n  \n  You’ve already had triplets \n  £0 \n  £0 \n  \n \n \n\n How you’re paid \n\n All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into your bank, building society or credit union account .
 You or your partner must also get one of these benefits: \n\n \n Income Support \n income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance \n income-related Employment and Support Allowance \n Pension Credit \n Child Tax Credit \n Working Tax Credit that includes a disability or severe disability element \n Universal Credit \n \n\n You may also qualify if you’re getting a Support for Mortgage Interest loan.
 \n\n \n If you live in Scotland you cannot get a Sure Start Maternity Grant.
 You can apply for a Pregnancy and Baby Payment instead.
 \n \n\n If you already have children under 16 \n\n You may still be able to get a grant if any of the following apply: \n\n \n you’re expecting a multiple birth (such as twins) \n the child you’re caring for is someone else’s (but not your partner’s) and the child was over 12 months old when the arrangement started \n you’ve been granted refugee status or humanitarian protection and you have a child or children from before you left your country \n \n\n \n You must claim by the deadline .
 \n \n\n If you’re not giving birth \n\n You may also be able to get a grant if you’re adopting or becoming a surrogate parent.
 \n\n The baby must be less than 1 year old on the date you claim.
 The latest you can claim is 6 months after your baby is born.
 \n\n If you’re becoming responsible for a child, you must claim within 6 months of this happening.
 \n\n \n Example \n\n If you’re adopting a child, you must claim within 6 months of the child being placed with you.
 \n \n\n Claim by post \n\n \n \n Print out and fill in the Sure Start Maternity Grant (SF100) claim form .
 \n \n \n Get a health professional such as a doctor or midwife to fill in the statement on page 10 of the form.
 You can send your form without this statement if necessary to meet the deadline.
 If you do this, you’ll be contacted about arranging the statement at a later date.
 \n \n \n Post the form to ‘Freepost DWP SSMG’ - you do not need a postcode or a stamp.
 \n \n \n\n \n There’s a different form and postal address if you live in Northern Ireland .
 \n \n\n You’ll get a letter telling you if your claim was successful.
 \n\n If you get Universal Credit, you will not get a decision on your claim until after your next payment.
 \n\n Get help with your claim \n\n Call the Sure Start Maternity Grant helpline.
 \n\n \n Sure Start Maternity Grant helpline \nTelephone: 0800 169 0140 \n Relay UK (if you cannot hear or speak on the phone): 18001 then 0800 169 0140 \n Video relay service for British Sign Language (BSL) users - check you can use the service \n Find out about call charges \n \n\n You can also contact Jobcentre Plus .
 \n\n Alternative formats \n\n Call the Sure Start Maternity Grant helpline to ask for alternative formats, such as braille, large print or audio CD.
 \n\n If you disagree with a decision \n You can challenge a decision about your claim.
 This is called asking for mandatory reconsideration.
